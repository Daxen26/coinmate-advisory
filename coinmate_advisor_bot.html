Coinmate Advisor — Guide how it works · the math · the columns · keys

How it runs (automatic)

  • Reads your live OKX P2P data — there is no Start button.
  • Runs every trading day 5:00 AM → 1:00 AM.
  • At 1:00 AM it finalizes the day: saves it to the Ledger folder (orders, profit & your ending PHP balance) and goes quiet.
  • 1:00–5:00 AM is downtime. At 5:00 AM it starts the new day itself and carries yesterday’s ending PHP balance as the new starting balance (nothing trades overnight).
  • End day now finishes early and does the same 1 AM finalize immediately.
  • Keep the helper window and this browser tab open for it to run 24/7.

Your ad (Coinmate panel)

  • Sell / Buy — your ad prices. Auto-detected from OKX when found, or type them in.
  • Wallet — your live USDT balance and its weighted-average buy cost.
  • PHP balance · PDAX — your real cash. Type it (auto-commas) and press Enter or ✓ to confirm. It carries overnight.
  • ≈ USDT — converts your PHP cash to USDT at the live market; Copy grabs the number for your ad.

Top competitors & the advisor

  • Top competitors — best 3 sellers (lowest) and buyers (highest) on Bank Transfer that meet your “Min competitor USDT” rule.
  • Advisor (middle) — tells you to raise / undercut / hold. Sell mode: “Hold for margin” (only margin-improving moves) vs “Undercut to sell fast”. It never goes below your Margin floor.
  • Fight merchant (Rules) — enter a rival nick for SELL and/or BUY. When that rival sits on top of you, a red Price war banner + sound tells you the exact move to win.

Recent orders · Daily Ledger · Profit

  • Recent orders — every order today with its exact rate, peso total, counterparty & payment method, straight from OKX. Click a rate to correct it manually (enter 0 to revert to OKX). ✕ removes an order from all the math.
  • Daily Ledger — USDT bought, sold, net position, order count and estimated fees. + manual logs an off-P2P entry (Credit = you bought USDT / paid out; Debit = a client paid you, with an optional name/note). + expense logs a business cost (PHP out) that lowers your profit. Every manual entry is listed below with ✎ edit and ✕ delete so you can fix a mistake and redo it.
  • Profit — your net profit for the day (sells spread − InstaPay − expenses). Click the profit number to set an audited figure — that needs the profit password.
  • Circulating capital — PHP cash + the peso value of your USDT.

The math

  • Rate & peso = the exact unitPrice and fiatAmount from each OKX order. No estimates.
  • Buy avg / Sell avg = total pesos ÷ total USDT (weighted), for today only.
  • InstaPay fee = ₱15 for every ₱50,000 bracket, on buys only.
  • Profit (today) = sold USDT × (Sell avg − Buy avg) − InstaPay fees − expenses. (An audited profit, if set, replaces this.)
  • End day now opens an audit popup: enter your final profit + PDAX cash (both protected by the profit password) to lock the day and carry the cash to 5 AM.
  • Wallet avg = live weighted-average price of the USDT you bought.
  • Cash moves with each new order during the day; it’s your confirmed PDAX cash as the source of truth.

Buttons

  • ⤓ scan day — import ALL of today’s completed OKX orders (use after a crash/outage), then confirm your cash.
  • ⤓ import — paste an OKX order list to override rates in bulk.
  • ⟲ start of day — manual reset: clears recent/today/profit and asks for your exact cash.
  • ↻ now — pull balance & orders immediately.
  • ⤓ export — save today into the Ledger folder right now.
  • + manual — log an off-P2P entry: Credit (you bought USDT / paid out) or Debit (a client paid you). + expense — a business cost that lowers profit.
  • End day now — finish the day early. − on any panel collapses it; + reopens.

Your ledger (Ledger folder)

  • Coinmate YYYY-MM-DD.xlsx — one Excel file per day: every transaction + the day summary + the ending PHP balance.
  • Coinmate Daily Summary.csv — one row per day (orders, bought/sold, averages, profit, fees, ending balance) — your whole history at a glance.

Keyboard

  • Enter — confirm the open popup (cash, manual entry, start-of-day).
  • Click a rate in Recent orders to edit it. That’s the only click-to-edit.
